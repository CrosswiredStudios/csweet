# Human Workforce and Engagements

## Purpose

Some work cannot or should not be completed entirely by AI.

CSweet should allow real people to:

- Join the marketplace
- Publish professional offerings
- Set rates
- Accept engagements
- Join company teams
- Receive tasks
- Submit deliverables
- Collaborate with agents
- Get paid

Humans participate in the same organizational model as digital workers, but they are not synchronous APIs and must never be treated as such.

## Professional profile

A human worker profile may contain:

- Display and legal identity
- Identity-verification status
- Location and time zone
- Languages
- Biography
- Skills and capability claims
- Portfolio and work history
- Availability
- Response-time expectations
- Preferred engagement models
- Rates
- Credentials and licenses
- Supported jurisdictions
- Reviews and reputation
- Dispute and completion history

One person may publish several offerings with different scope and rates.

Example:

```text
Jane Smith

Offerings:
- Fractional CFO — $175/hour
- Startup financial review — $1,500 fixed price
- Monthly finance leadership — $3,500/month
- Fundraising model review — $750 fixed price
```

## Engagement models

Initial supported models should include:

- Hourly
- Fixed-price task
- Milestone project
- Monthly retainer
- Consultation
- Packaged recurring service

Later models may include:

- Weekly capacity
- Outcome-based pricing
- Agency teams
- Employer-of-record relationships

## Engagement lifecycle

Suggested statuses:

- Recommended
- Invited
- ProposalSubmitted
- Negotiating
- AwaitingCompanyApproval
- AwaitingProfessionalAcceptance
- Active
- Paused
- Completed
- Terminated
- Disputed

A human becomes an active company staff member only after both parties accept engagement terms.

## Engagement contract

The contract should record:

- Parties and marketplace identities
- Engagement type
- Scope
- Rate or fixed price
- Maximum hours or total cost
- Milestones
- Start and end dates
- Deliverables
- Acceptance criteria
- Revision allowance
- Communication expectations
- Confidentiality and data access
- Intellectual-property terms
- Expense policy
- Cancellation and refund terms
- Jurisdiction and professional limitations

## Hiring workflow

1. A planner identifies a capability gap.
2. The workforce router searches available professionals.
3. The Personal Assistant compares capability, cost, credentials, location, reviews, and availability.
4. The CEO or delegated manager approves outreach or an engagement limit.
5. The professional accepts, declines, or counterproposes.
6. Both sides accept final scope and terms.
7. Required funds are reserved or authorized.
8. The professional is added to the company with scoped access.
9. Tasks and responsibilities are assigned.

## Human task lifecycle

```text
Task proposed
→ Professional notified
→ Accepted, declined, or counterproposed
→ Scheduled
→ In progress
→ Questions or blockers
→ Deliverables submitted
→ Review and revisions
→ Accepted
→ Payment released
```

Human tasks may require:

- Due dates and working windows
- Estimated and maximum hours
- Meetings
- Physical location
- Materials and shipping
- Expense policy
- Milestones
- Revision rounds
- Acceptance period

## Professional portal

A human worker needs a dedicated experience showing:

- Engagement invitations
- Assigned companies
- Active responsibilities
- Tasks and deadlines
- Messages and mentions
- Meetings
- Authorized files and project context
- Time entries
- Expenses
- Milestones
- Deliverables
- Approval status
- Earnings and payouts

## AI-to-human delegation

Agents should prepare structured work orders rather than sending vague messages.

A work order includes:

- Objective
- Scope
- Inputs
- Deliverables
- Acceptance criteria
- Deadline
- Budget
- Dependencies
- Credential requirements
- Communication expectations

When an agent escalates to a human, it should package:

- Work already completed
- Unresolved questions
- Relevant artifacts
- Clear reason human participation is needed
- Estimated human time
- Budget impact

## Human-to-agent delegation

Authorized professionals may delegate supporting work to company agents.

Examples:

- Accountant asks a document agent to organize receipts
- Designer asks an asset agent to convert file formats
- Attorney asks a research agent to assemble public sources
- Project manager asks agents to produce status summaries
- Photographer asks an image-processing agent to prepare export sizes

A human’s presence in the company does not automatically grant access to every agent, tool, data source, or department.

## Time tracking

Hourly work should support:

- Manual or timer-based entries
- Task reference
- Description
- Start and end time
- Billable status
- Weekly and task limits
- Submission and approval
- Dispute status

Activity surveillance should not be required by default.

The system should focus on deliverables, agreed time records, and transparent approvals.

## Milestones and fixed-price work

A milestone includes:

- Deliverable
- Amount
- Due date
- Acceptance criteria
- Review period
- Revision rules
- Funding status
- Release status

Payments should release only after:

- Acceptance
- Automatic acceptance under agreed terms
- Dispute resolution

## Expenses

Labor and expenses should have separate limits.

Policies may require:

- Preapproval
- Receipts
- Per-expense threshold
- Category restrictions
- Travel rules
- Markup rules
- Reimbursement timeline

## Credentials and regulated roles

A role name does not prove qualification.

Credential records should capture:

- Credential type
- Issuer
- Jurisdiction
- Verification source
- Verification level
- License number where appropriate
- Expiration
- Permitted service scope

CSweet should show whether a credential is:

- Self-declared
- Provider-asserted
- Marketplace-verified
- Directly verified with an issuer

## Engagement classification

The friendly UI may use “staff” and “hire,” but the underlying relationship must be explicit:

- Independent professional
- Agency contractor
- Managed service
- Provider employee
- Actual company employee

The platform should not guarantee worker classification based on labels alone.

Legal and tax requirements vary by jurisdiction and need dedicated review before marketplace launch.

## Privacy and access

Human access should be:

- Engagement-scoped
- Least-privilege
- Time-limited
- Revocable
- Audited
- Separated by company and project

Viewing, downloading, editing, exporting, and resharing should be separately controllable where practical.

## Reputation

Potential dimensions:

- Quality
- Communication
- Timeliness
- Professionalism
- Scope accuracy
- Budget accuracy
- Completion rate
- Rehire rate
- Dispute rate
- Credential standing

AI-generated assessments should not automatically become public ratings without transparent evidence and human review.

## Hybrid teams

The platform should support teams where people supervise agents or agents coordinate people.

Example:

```text
Finance Director — local agent
├── Bookkeeper — remote provider worker
├── Financial Analyst — local agent
└── CPA — human professional
```

The system should optimize human time toward judgment, review, accountability, relationships, and physical work rather than information gathering and repetitive preparation.
