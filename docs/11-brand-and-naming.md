# Brand and Naming Notes

## Current working name

`CSweet` is the current repository and working project name.

The name is a play on “C-suite,” which fits the core experience of acting as the CEO and communicating through an executive assistant.

Potential strengths:

- Memorable
- Friendly
- Executive-oriented
- Works well with product areas such as CSweet Workforce and CSweet Marketplace
- Fits the company-simulation personality

Potential concerns:

- Similarity to existing uses of “C-Sweet”
- Search and trademark collision risk
- May require explanation when spoken
- Could be seen as informal for regulated professional workflows

Formal trademark and domain review should happen before public launch.

## Names discussed

### CSweet

Best conceptual fit among the original options, but requires clearance.

### CSuite

Clear meaning, but highly generic and difficult to own in search or trademark.

### SeaSuite

Professional sounding, but suggests marine or hospitality software.

### SeaSweet

Memorable, but sounds more like food, candy, or a consumer brand.

### EnterPrize

Clever combination of enterprise, entry, prize, and game progression.

Strengths:

- Playful
- Company-building association
- Good simulation feel

Concerns:

- May sound like a contest or giveaway
- Less directly connected to workforce orchestration
- Spelling may be inconsistent
- Existing uses may create confusion

### Hirearchy

A strong conceptual combination of hiring and hierarchy.

Strengths:

- Explains staffing and org structure
- Memorable
- Fits the game-like experience

Concerns:

- Looks like a misspelling
- May be too playful for professional services

### StaffForge

Strong professional workforce brand.

Strengths:

- Clear
- Extensible
- Fits marketplace and team assembly

Concerns:

- Less connected to the CEO experience
- Industrial tone

### OrgPilot

Strong professional and executive-oriented option.

Strengths:

- Conveys organizational guidance and orchestration
- Works for serious business use
- Flexible beyond startups

Concerns:

- “Pilot” and “copilot” naming is crowded
- Less game-like

## Naming criteria

A final name should be evaluated on:

- Memorability
- Pronunciation
- Spelling
- Searchability
- Trademark risk
- Domain availability
- Open-source project fit
- Professional credibility
- Game and simulation personality
- Relevance to mixed human and agent work
- Ability to support marketplace and provider brands

## Possible positioning lines

- Turn an idea into a company.
- Build a company that works.
- Run an agent-first company.
- Hire agents. Add experts. Build anything.
- Where ideas become companies.
- Your idea. Your company. Your workforce.
- Agents first. Humans where they matter most.

## Recommendation

Continue using `CSweet` as the working codename and repository name during prototyping.

Do not make a major public branding investment until:

1. The product scope is more stable.
2. Domain options are reviewed.
3. Trademark clearance is performed.
4. The balance between serious business software and simulation is validated with users.
